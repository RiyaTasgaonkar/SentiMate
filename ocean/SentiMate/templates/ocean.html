OCEAN Model

In the OCEAN model, people are understood to have varying levels of key personality traits which fuel our actions. Though personality traits cannot specifically predict behavior, differences in these traits help us to understand why people react differently, behave differently, and see things differently from others in the same situation.

OCEAN personality Traits

The Big Five personality traits are extraversion, agreeableness, openness, conscientiousness, and neuroticism. Each trait represents a continuum. Individuals can fall anywhere on the continuum for each trait.

Openness


Openness includes traits like being insightful and imaginative and having a wide variety of interests. They enjoy discovering novel experiences and like to learn new things. Openness is strongly related to a person’s interest in art and culture.

Conscientiousness


Conscientiousness is a trait that can be described as the tendency to control impulses and behaviors that facilitate goal-directed actions. Conscientious people excel in working within the rules, and plan and organize effectively.

Extraversion


Extraversion easures the level of sociability. It concerns where an individual draws energy from. Extraversion includes the traits of energetic, talkative, and assertive.

Agreableness


Agreeableness measures how well you get along with people. Are you considerate, helpful and willing to compromise? They make affectionalte friends and loved ones.

Neuroticism


Neuroticism describes a person’s tendency to experience negative emotions, including fear, sadness, anxiety, guilt, and shame. Do you react negatively or calmly to bad news? Do you worry obsessively about small details, or are you relaxed in stressful situations? While everyone experiences these emotions from time to time, some people are more prone to them than others.
